Born from personal frustration, this project evolved into a comprehensive proposal for the city of Ohrid: a digital parking solution designed to improve user experience while significantly boosting city revenue through streamlined payment processes.

Problem Statement

  • Tourists often avoided fines, leading to significant revenue losses for the city

  • Locals found SMS payment process slow, unreliable, and frustrating

  • Outdated payment infrastructure created friction for all user groups

Goals

City Impact

Increase revenue by reducing unpaid fines and streamlining collection

User Experience

Make payments quick, intuitive, and completely error-free

Technical Goals

Build a lean, scalable MVP to test market feasibility

Design Process

01

Research

Conducted in-depth interviews with drivers, analyzed the existing SMS system, and observed behavioral patterns across technical and non-technical user groups.

02

Design Iterations

Evolved from initial sketches to refined user flows, ultimately pivoting to a lean MVP approach focused on core payment functionality and stakeholder priorities.

03

Challenges

Initial version was rejected for being too user-focused. Successfully realigned with stakeholder revenue goals while managing scope creep and maintaining user experience quality.

04

Development & Testing

Built complete application with admin dashboard. Conducted extensive testing with diverse user groups including technical, non-technical, and elderly users achieving 90%+ success rates.

Results & Impact

Fully Functional MVP

Delivered a complete, working prototype with all core features implemented.

User Validation

Tested with 10+ diverse users across different demographics and tech proficiency.

High Success Rate

90%+ of test users completed payments without assistance.
