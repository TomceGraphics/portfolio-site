The 120-Second
Bottleneck

Ohrid, a UNESCO heritage city, was losing thousands in revenue. Why? Because paying for parking was harder than avoiding the fine.

  • Revenue Leak: Tourists couldn't use the SMS-only system, leading to abandoned payments.
  • High Friction: Locals faced a sluggish 2-minute process prone to network errors.

Retrospective

Revenue > UX?

I learned that for stakeholders, UX is a means to an end. By pivoting the pitch from "prettier interface" to "revenue recovery," buy-in was immediate.

The MVP Trap

Initial designs were feature-bloated. Cutting 40% of the features actually increased the payment success rate by removing distractions.
